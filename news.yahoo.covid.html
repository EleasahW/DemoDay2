    • Pandemic burnout causing teachers to rethink returning in the fall

    • Many teachers across the nation said the stress of managing their own health concerns alongside online and in-person learning has pushed them to a breaking point

    • A survey of educators from January found that one-quarter of the U.S teachers plan to leave the profession by the end of the year due to pandemic-related anxiety.

    • Henry Seton, a ninth-grade English teacher from Columbus, Ohio, said that the pandemic's disruption to in-person education was the main factor in his decision to take the year off

    • Educators Seton had spoken to described "increased secondary trauma for teachers ... working with students who are living through trauma brought on by COVID," he said.
